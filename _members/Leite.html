Current Research Summary:

The measurement of the refractive index of solid samples has been implemented for many years, however with the continuous development of new types of materials and new possible applications, some techniques are no longer as viable or efficient. Existing techniques for determining the refractive index that produce results with a high accuracy, in the order of 10-5, involve the construction of optical prisms with very tight manufacturing tolerances and are associated to very high manufacturing costs. The challenge proposed for this master thesis is the development of a cost-effective, simple to implement and automated system capable of producing reliable results with an uncertainty below 10-2, on optically simple samples. A HeNe laser, a precision stage and a CCD camera are used, the latter two controlled by LabVIEW code.
Data collected by the lateral displacement of the laser in the CCD allowed the achievement of measurements of the refractive index of glass samples (around 10 mm thick) with an uncertainty of 10-3 and an error typically of 10-3, lower for calibration samples (10-4)
